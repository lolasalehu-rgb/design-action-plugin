I've been a product designer working across multiple streams for years — and the hardest part was never the design work itself.

It was keeping track of everything.

Decisions from Monday's PM sync. Pain points from Tuesday's usability test. Feature requests buried in Wednesday's Slack thread. A stakeholder quote from last week's sprint review that I know was important but can't quite remember.

By Friday, I'd spend an hour just reconstructing what happened earlier that week.

So I built something to fix it.

**design-action** is a plugin that turns scattered design evidence into grounded artifacts — with full source citations.

Here's what it does:

1. **Gathers** evidence from your meetings, task tracker, chat, and design tools
2. **Synthesizes** pain points, decisions, quotes, and open questions across sources
3. **Suggests** the right artifact type (journey map, wireframe, research synthesis, etc.)
4. **Creates** the artifact with every element citing its source
5. **Tracks** design decisions and keeps your backlog updated

The key insight: every element in the artifact traces back to where it was discussed. No hallucinated design rationale. No "I think someone mentioned this" — actual citations.

It works with the tools you already use:
→ Meetings: Granola, Otter.ai, Fireflies, Google Meet
→ Tasks: Jira, Linear, GitHub Issues
→ Chat: Slack, Discord, Teams
→ Design: Figma, Penpot

You get value on day one with just meeting notes. Scale to automated daily briefings when you're ready.

It's free for personal use. If you're a product designer, design consultant, or design lead navigating cross-functional complexity — give it a try:
